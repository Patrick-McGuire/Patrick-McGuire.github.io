Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution:

CUREE Coral Reef Monitoring Robot

Overview

For summers 2022 and 2023, I worked in Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution's Autonomous Robotics and Perception laboratory helping develop CUREE. CUREE is an autonomous robot for intelligent coral reef exploration and mapping. It is used to develop machine learning in underwater robotics and create high-accuracy visual maps of coral reefs.

I worked on the electrical system, sensor integration, and operations. I designed second and third-generation electrical systems, including multiple custom circuit boards. I wrote a ROS driver for a Doppler Velocity Log and integrated it mechanically.  In the summer of 2023, I went to the U.S. Virgin Islands for two weeks of fieldwork, where I operated and maintained CUREE.
