Physics core

p(x,y,f) = sum_i P_i,rms(1m) * D_i(theta,f) * 10^(-A(f) r_i,km / 20) / r_i * exp(j(k r_i + phi_i)) SPL = 20 log10(|p_rms| / p_ref) I = p_rms^2 / (rho c) Default p_ref = 1e-6 Pa, the underwater dB re 1 uPa reference.

The field is a horizontal 2D slice of a 3D free-field solution. It uses spherical spreading, coherent phase addition, selectable directional source patterns, and optional absorption. Chirp SPL plots average pressure power across sampled frequencies. Boundaries, reflections, reverberation, nonlinear source behavior, electrical drive limits, and near-field transducer structure are not included.

Absorption

The Thorp option estimates seawater absorption in dB/km as a function of frequency in kHz. Around 9 kHz it is about 1 dB/km, so geometric spreading usually dominates from 1 m to 1 km.

CPT-2521C-500 starting point

DigiKey lists the part as a Same Sky piezo transducer, 25.60 mm diameter, 3.7 kHz, 95 dB at 20 V and 30 cm, IP68. The simulator includes that geometry as a first pattern preset. At 9 kHz in water a 25.6 mm piston is much smaller than one wavelength, so its directivity is broad. The published SPL is an air measurement and is not a water-source-level calibration.
